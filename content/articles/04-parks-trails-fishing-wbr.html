Parks, Trails, and Fishing in West Baton Rouge Parish

June 3, 2026 Outdoors & Recreation

West Baton Rouge Parish may cover only 204 square miles, but it offers a surprising range of outdoor recreation for a parish its size. From manicured community parks with splash pads and ball fields to the wild expanse of the Atchafalaya Basin just to the south, WBR gives residents options for staying active year-round. Here is what is available and where to find it.

Parish Parks Overview

West Baton Rouge Parish Parks and Recreation operates and maintains parks and facilities across all three communities: Port Allen, Brusly, and Addis. The department also manages six community centers (two in Port Allen, two in Addis, one in Brusly, and one in Erwinville) and runs sports leagues, youth programs, and community events throughout the year.

The main office is located at the Port Allen Community Center, 749 N. Jefferson Ave., Port Allen, LA 70767.

Parks with Facilities

Seven parks in the parish have pavilions, recreation halls, or other facilities available for rental:

Alexander Park (Brusly)

Alexander Park is one of the most popular parks in the parish, especially for families with young children. It features a pavilion available for rental, baseball fields, a splash pad, and playground equipment. The splash pad draws crowds during the summer months and gives kids a way to cool off without leaving the parish.

Rivault Park (Port Allen)

Located in Port Allen, Rivault Park offers a pavilion available for rental, a baseball field, and open green space. It is a solid option for family gatherings, birthday parties, and casual recreation.

Myhand Park

Myhand Park is another facility with rental options, providing covered pavilion space for events along with recreational amenities.

Additional Facilities

Erwinville Park, Lukeville Park, the Soccer Complex, and Williams and Lee Park round out the list of parks with bookable facilities. Each offers different combinations of pavilions, concession stands, and sports fields.

Open Parks

Three parks operate without rental facilities but are open to the public for casual use: Mulatto Bend Park, Rev. Manuel Park, and Winterville Park. These are quieter spots for walking, picnicking, or just getting outside without the organized activity of the larger parks.

Walking Trails

The parish maintains walking tracks throughout WBR that are open to the public. These trails include bathroom facilities and exercise pads, making them practical for daily fitness routines rather than just occasional use. The flat terrain of the Mississippi River floodplain means the trails are accessible to walkers and joggers of all fitness levels.

For residents who want more extensive trail networks, BREC (Baton Rouge Recreation Commission) maintains over 180 parks across the river in East Baton Rouge, including Frenchtown Conservation Area, the largest park in Baton Rouge at nearly 500 acres. It is a short drive across the bridge for weekend hikes or longer outings.

The Dog Park

WBR Parks and Recreation opened a dog park located next to the football field behind Port Allen Middle School. The park is divided into two sections: one for small dogs and one for large dogs. It is fenced, free to use, and fills a gap that dog owners in the parish had been vocal about for years.

Williams and Lee Pool

The Williams and Lee Pool at 1631 Louisiana Ave., Port Allen, is the parish's public swimming facility. Owned and operated by WBR Parks and Recreation, the pool is open free to the public from Memorial Day weekend through the first week of August.

A free public pool is increasingly rare in Louisiana, and Williams and Lee is a genuine community asset. It gets busy on hot afternoons, especially in June and July, but the price (free) and the convenience make it the default summer destination for families across the parish.

Port Allen Community Center

The Port Allen Community Center is more than just a meeting hall. The facility includes a commercial kitchen fully equipped for catering, a wet bar with ice maker, and a built-in AV system for presentations and events. Outside, it has a playground, splash pad, and two tennis courts available for community use, plus a large covered pavilion with a concession stand and restrooms.

The community center hosts parish events, private rentals, and serves as a hub for Parks and Recreation programming. It is one of the most versatile public facilities in West Baton Rouge.

Fishing: The Atchafalaya Basin

The single greatest outdoor asset near West Baton Rouge Parish is the Atchafalaya Basin, the largest river swamp in the United States. Stretching south from the parish through Iberville and St. Martin parishes, the basin covers over 260,000 acres of swamps, bayous, and bottomland hardwood forests.

Access points along I-10 near Addis put WBR residents within minutes of some of the best freshwater fishing in North America. The basin produces largemouth bass, crappie (sac-a-lait in local parlance), catfish, bream, and crawfish. During crawfish season, the basin is the source of much of the crawfish consumed across South Louisiana.

Bank fishing and boat launches are available at multiple points. Local knowledge matters here; water levels, seasonal patterns, and specific bayou access change throughout the year. The WBR community has deep roots in basin fishing, and newcomers will find that asking around at local bait shops or the community center is the fastest way to learn productive spots.

The Mississippi River Levee

The Mississippi River levee runs along the eastern edge of the parish, and while it is not an official trail, it functions as one. Walking, jogging, and cycling on the levee is a long-standing local tradition. The elevated path provides views of the river and the Baton Rouge skyline, and it is one of the few places in the parish where you can get a genuine workout on an incline.

The levee is maintained by the Army Corps of Engineers and is accessible at multiple points in Port Allen. It is not lit at night and has no facilities, so plan accordingly.

Sports Leagues and Youth Programs

WBR Parks and Recreation runs youth and adult sports leagues throughout the year, including baseball, softball, football, soccer, and basketball. The Soccer Complex and various park ball fields host games and practices during their respective seasons. Registration information and schedules are available through the parish recreation website at wbrrecreation.org or through the REC1 online catalog.

For a small parish, the outdoor recreation options in West Baton Rouge are solid and well-maintained. The combination of parish parks, the free public pool, the dog park, and proximity to the Atchafalaya Basin gives residents a range of options that larger communities sometimes struggle to match.
