WBR School Ratings Guide 2026: What Parents Need to Know

June 3, 2026 Education

School quality is the number one reason families choose West Baton Rouge Parish, and specifically, the number one reason they choose Brusly over Port Allen. Understanding the differences between the two feeder patterns, what the ratings actually mean, and what options exist beyond the parish is essential for any family making a housing decision in WBR.

District at a Glance

West Baton Rouge Parish Public Schools is a single district serving the entire parish. Key numbers for the 2025-2026 school year:

  • Total enrollment: approximately 4,378 students
  • Number of schools: 10 (6 elementary, 2 middle, 2 high)
  • Student-teacher ratio: 14:1 district-wide
  • District Performance Score: 86.7
  • Graduation rate: 89% (up from 83% five years ago)
  • Math proficiency: 35% (Louisiana average: 34%)
  • Reading proficiency: 41% (Louisiana average: 43%)
  • Minority enrollment: 61% (Louisiana average: 59%)
  • Public school enrollment rate: 89% of parish K-12 students (vs. 85% statewide)

The district ranks 45th out of 177 school districts in Louisiana, placing it in the top 30% statewide. The average school ranking across the parish is 7 out of 10.

The Brusly Schools

Lukeville Elementary School

Lukeville Elementary is the crown jewel of the district. It ranks in the top 12% of all Louisiana elementary schools and has earned 4 out of 5 stars from rating services. This is the school that draws families to Brusly. If school quality is your top priority and you are looking at WBR, Lukeville's attendance zone should be your first research stop.

Brusly Elementary School

Brusly Elementary serves PreK through 1st grade with approximately 518 students and a student-teacher ratio of 11:1. Niche gives it an A- grade. The school enrolls 71% economically disadvantaged students, a reminder that WBR schools serve diverse populations regardless of neighborhood reputation. There are 46 full-time equivalent teachers and one full-time counselor on staff.

Brusly Middle School

Brusly Middle consistently ranks among the top middle schools in the state. It maintains the strong academic culture established at the elementary level and feeds into Brusly High School.

Brusly High School

Brusly High enrolls 734 students with a 13:1 student-teacher ratio and holds a B+ grade from Niche based on 97 user reviews averaging 3.9 stars. The school regularly appears in statewide top-school rankings. Beyond academics, Brusly High is known for its competitive athletics programs, particularly football and baseball, and the school serves as a social anchor for the Brusly community.

The Port Allen Schools

Port Allen Elementary Schools

Port Allen has multiple elementary schools feeding into Port Allen Middle School. These schools serve different neighborhoods within Port Allen and tend to have higher percentages of economically disadvantaged students than their Brusly counterparts.

Port Allen Middle School

Port Allen Middle School has lower proficiency rates compared to Brusly Middle. It is one of the most common comparison points for families evaluating the two communities.

Port Allen High School

Port Allen High enrolls approximately 457 students in grades 9-12 with a 12:1 student-teacher ratio, which is notably lower than the state average of 17:1. Math proficiency rates of 35-39% are actually above the state average of 32%, while reading proficiency at 25-29% falls below the state average of 42%. Minority enrollment is 77% of the student body.

Port Allen High receives less favorable ratings than Brusly High on most review platforms. However, the smaller class sizes and above-average math scores suggest that students who are engaged receive meaningful individual attention.

The Performance Gap: Context Matters

The performance difference between the Brusly and Port Allen feeder patterns is real and consistent across multiple measures. Brusly schools outperform Port Allen schools on state tests, graduation rates, and third-party ratings. This drives housing demand in Brusly and contributes to higher home prices in Brusly attendance zones.

However, context matters. Socioeconomic factors, school resources, and teaching quality all contribute to these differences. Port Allen schools serve a population with higher rates of economic disadvantage, and direct school-to-school comparisons that ignore that context are incomplete.

Both sets of schools benefit from the district's overall small size. With only 4,378 students across the entire parish, individual students are less likely to fall through the cracks than in larger districts. The district-wide graduation rate of 89% reflects this.

Private and Alternative Options

There are no private schools located within West Baton Rouge Parish. Families seeking private education cross the bridge into Baton Rouge, where options include:

  • Episcopal High School (college prep, co-ed)
  • Catholic High School (all-boys)
  • St. Joseph's Academy (all-girls)
  • Dunham School (PreK-12, co-ed)
  • Parkview Baptist School (PreK-12, co-ed)

These schools add the bridge commute to the daily routine, which is a meaningful factor during rush hour. Some WBR families solve this by having one parent drive the kids across early, before peak traffic.

What to Look For Beyond Ratings

School ratings from Niche, GreatSchools, and Public School Review provide useful starting points, but they do not tell the full story. When evaluating WBR schools, consider:

  • Visit during a school day. Walk the hallways, observe classrooms, and talk to teachers. The culture of a school is hard to capture in a rating number.
  • Check extracurricular offerings. Band, athletics, and clubs matter for student engagement, especially at the middle and high school level.
  • Ask about special education services. Small districts sometimes struggle with resources for specialized needs. Ask directly about the services your child might require.
  • Talk to other parents. The WBR parent community is tight-knit. Join local Facebook groups or attend a school event to hear honest opinions from people with kids in the system.
  • Look at trends, not snapshots. The district's graduation rate climbing from 83% to 89% over five years is arguably more meaningful than any single test score.

The Bottom Line for Families

If school quality is your primary driver, Brusly is the clear choice within WBR. Lukeville Elementary, Brusly Middle, and Brusly High form one of the strongest public school feeder patterns in the greater Baton Rouge metro area, and you get it without the property taxes of East Baton Rouge neighborhoods with comparable schools.

Port Allen schools are not bad schools. They face challenges that correlate with the demographics they serve, and they offer advantages, particularly small class sizes, that get overlooked in ratings-focused comparisons.

For the most current information, visit the West Baton Rouge Parish Schools website at wbrschools.net, or check individual school profiles on Niche, GreatSchools, or Public School Review.
