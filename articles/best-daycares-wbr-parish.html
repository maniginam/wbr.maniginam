Childcare in WBR: What You Need to Know

Finding reliable childcare is one of the first challenges families face when they move to West Baton Rouge Parish -- or when they start a family here. The good news is that WBR has a solid network of licensed childcare centers, church-based programs, and family child care homes that serve families across Port Allen, Brusly, and Addis. The less good news is that waitlists are real, especially for infant care, so starting your search early is essential.

Louisiana requires all childcare providers caring for more than two unrelated children to be licensed through the Louisiana Department of Education (LDE). Every licensed center receives regular inspections, and you can look up any center's inspection history online at the LDE website. This is the single most important step you can take before enrolling your child anywhere -- check the licensing record first.

West Baton Rouge is a small parish, which means your childcare options are more limited than what you would find across the river in Baton Rouge. But that smaller scale comes with advantages. The centers here tend to have lower child-to-staff ratios, the staff turnover is lower because employees often live in the community, and the directors know families by name. When your child's teacher sees you at Bergeron's on Saturday morning and asks how little one is doing, that is the WBR childcare experience in a nutshell.

Port Allen Daycares

Port Allen Day Care Center

437 Court St, Port Allen, LA 70767 -- (225) 344-8225

Port Allen Day Care has been a fixture on Court Street for years, serving children from infants through pre-kindergarten. The center sits in the heart of Port Allen, making it convenient for parents who work in the parish seat or commute across the bridge to Baton Rouge. Drop-off on Court Street is straightforward, and you can be on I-10 within minutes.

The center operates Monday through Friday, typically from 6:30 AM to 5:30 PM, which covers the standard workday with enough buffer on both ends for parents with commutes. Staff ratios follow Louisiana requirements: one adult per five infants, one per eight toddlers, and one per twelve preschoolers. Parents report that the staff is consistent -- you see the same faces week after week, which matters when your child is building trust with caregivers.

The curriculum follows a structured daily schedule with circle time, art activities, outdoor play, and rest periods. For pre-K children, the program incorporates school readiness skills that align with what WBR Parish elementary schools expect when kids start kindergarten.

Ages served: 6 weeks to 5 years

Hours: Monday - Friday, 6:30 AM - 5:30 PM

Estimated cost: $150 - $185/week depending on age

West Baton Rouge Head Start

855 N Alexander Ave, Port Allen, LA 70767 -- (225) 346-1786

Head Start is a federally funded program serving children ages 3 to 5 from income-qualifying families, and the West Baton Rouge location on North Alexander Avenue is one of the strongest Head Start programs in the region. The program is free for families who qualify, and it provides far more than babysitting. Children receive structured early education, developmental screenings, dental and health checkups, and nutritious meals -- breakfast, lunch, and a snack -- at no cost.

The WBR Head Start program follows the Creative Curriculum, which is play-based but academically grounded. Teachers are required to have at least an associate's degree in early childhood education, and many have bachelor's degrees. The program also connects families with social services, including housing assistance, job training referrals, and mental health support.

Enrollment is based on income eligibility, with priority given to families below the federal poverty line. Foster children and children with disabilities automatically qualify. Applications typically open in spring for the following school year, but the center maintains a waitlist year-round.

Ages served: 3 to 5 years

Hours: Monday - Friday, school-day schedule (varies by site)

Cost: Free for qualifying families

Faith-Based Options in Port Allen

First Baptist Church of Port Allen at 645 Court St offers a Mother's Day Out program and preschool classes during the school year. This is a popular option for families who want a faith-based environment with a part-time schedule. The program runs two or three days per week, which works well for parents who only need part-time care or who want to supplement a home-based arrangement. The church's location on Court Street puts it within walking distance of Port Allen Day Care, so families often compare the two before deciding.

Grace Baptist Church on LA-1 has offered children's programs historically, though availability varies by year. Call ahead at (225) 343-4422 to check current programming.

Brusly Daycares

Little Sprouts Learning Center

177 S Kirkland Dr, Brusly, LA 70719 -- (225) 749-3999

Little Sprouts is one of the newer childcare options in the parish, and it has quickly become a favorite among Brusly families. The facility on South Kirkland Drive is purpose-built for childcare, with age-appropriate classrooms, a secure outdoor play area, and modern safety features including keypad entry and camera systems. The building itself feels bright and clean -- not always a given with childcare centers -- and the outdoor space has shade structures that matter in Louisiana's summers.

The curriculum emphasizes school readiness through structured play, literacy activities, and social-emotional development. Children are grouped by age, and the transition from the toddler room to the preschool room is handled gradually so kids adjust without the trauma of a sudden change. Parents particularly appreciate the daily reports they receive via app, which include photos, meal tracking, nap times, and notes from teachers.

Brusly parents with children at Cohn Elementary often choose Little Sprouts because the center offers an after-school pickup program. Staff picks children up from school and brings them back to the center, where they can do homework, have a snack, and play until parents arrive.

Ages served: 6 weeks to 12 years (including after-school care)

Hours: Monday - Friday, 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM

Estimated cost: $140 - $195/week

Brusly United Methodist Church Children's Program

200 S Vaughan St, Brusly, LA 70719 -- (225) 749-2792

Brusly United Methodist runs a well-regarded preschool and Mother's Day Out program during the school year. The program focuses on social development, creative play, and Bible-based character education. Classes are small -- typically eight to ten children per group -- and the teachers are experienced, with many having served in the program for years. The church facility on South Vaughan Street is centrally located in Brusly, close to Brusly Elementary and the Brusly Town Hall.

This is a part-time program (typically Tuesday through Thursday, mornings), so it works best for families with a stay-at-home parent who wants structured socialization for their child, or for families who can piece together care with grandparents or other arrangements on the off days.

Ages served: 2 to 4 years

Schedule: School year only, 2-3 days per week

Estimated cost: $100 - $140/month

Addis Daycares

First Baptist Church Daycare of Addis

7400 Hwy 1 South, Addis, LA 70710 -- (225) 749-2422

First Baptist Addis operates a full-day childcare program that has served the southern end of the parish for decades. The daycare is housed in the church's education wing, which gives children access to spacious indoor areas and a large outdoor playground. The program runs year-round, Monday through Friday, and accepts children from infants through kindergarten age.

The faith-based curriculum incorporates Bible stories and character lessons alongside standard early childhood education. Parents who value a Christian environment consistently rate this program highly. The staff is drawn largely from the local congregation and the surrounding community, which creates a family atmosphere. If you are new to Addis and want your child in a setting where the caregivers know the community inside and out, this is a strong option.

The location on Hwy 1 South is convenient for families who commute along the LA-1 corridor to Baton Rouge or to the industrial plants south of Addis.

Ages served: 6 weeks to 5 years

Hours: Monday - Friday, 6:30 AM - 5:30 PM

Estimated cost: $125 - $170/week

Family Child Care Homes in Addis

Addis has several licensed family child care homes -- providers who care for children in their own residence. Louisiana allows family child care providers to care for up to six children (or up to twelve with an assistant). These homes offer a more intimate setting than a center, with mixed-age groups that can feel more like an extended family environment.

Family child care homes are particularly popular in Addis because the town's residential layout makes them accessible from most neighborhoods. Costs tend to be slightly lower than center-based care, typically $100 to $150 per week. The trade-off is that hours may be less flexible, and if the provider gets sick, you need a backup plan. You can search for licensed family child care homes in the Addis area through the Louisiana Department of Education's provider search tool.

Head Start & Pre-K Programs

Beyond the Head Start center on North Alexander Avenue in Port Allen, West Baton Rouge Parish participates in Louisiana's LA 4 Pre-K program, which provides free pre-kindergarten for four-year-olds. LA 4 classrooms are located within WBR Parish elementary schools, including Brusly Elementary and Port Allen Elementary. These are full-day programs that run on the school calendar and follow the same curriculum standards as the regular school system.

The combination of Head Start (income-based, ages 3-5) and LA 4 (universal for four-year-olds) means that many WBR families can access free or low-cost pre-kindergarten. The application process for LA 4 typically opens in the spring through the WBR Parish School Board office at 3761 Rosedale Road in Port Allen. Call (225) 343-8517 for enrollment information.

Tip: If your child turns four by September 30, they qualify for LA 4 regardless of family income. Apply early -- these spots fill up, and transportation is limited.

Louisiana Licensing & How to Check

Every licensed childcare center and family child care home in Louisiana is inspected by the Louisiana Department of Education's Division of Licensing. Inspection reports are public records, and checking them is the most important thing you can do before enrolling your child anywhere.

To look up a provider's record:

  1. Visit the LDE Child Care Provider Search at ldh.la.gov (search for "child care provider search")
  2. Filter by parish (West Baton Rouge) or enter the provider's name
  3. Review the inspection history, including any deficiencies cited and how they were corrected
  4. Check the provider's license type (Class A or Class B) and capacity

Class A licenses are the standard for childcare centers and require compliance with all Louisiana regulations, including staff-to-child ratios, health and safety standards, background checks, and curriculum requirements. Class B licenses are for family child care homes and have a different set of requirements suited to home-based care.

A clean inspection report is a green light. A report with multiple deficiencies or repeated violations is a red flag. Pay particular attention to deficiencies related to supervision, safety hazards, and background check compliance.

What Daycare Costs in WBR

Childcare in West Baton Rouge Parish generally costs less than in East Baton Rouge, Ascension, or Livingston parishes. Here is what you can expect to pay in 2026:

Age Group Center-Based (weekly) Family Home (weekly)
Infants (6 weeks - 12 months) $170 - $200 $130 - $160
Toddlers (1 - 2 years) $155 - $185 $120 - $150
Preschool (3 - 4 years) $135 - $170 $110 - $140
After-school (5 - 12 years) $75 - $110 $60 - $90

Most centers charge a registration fee ($25 - $75) and require a supply fee once or twice a year ($30 - $50). Some charge extra for meals; others include meals in the weekly rate. Always ask about the full cost breakdown before enrolling.

Child Care Assistance Program (CCAP)

Louisiana's CCAP helps income-eligible families pay for childcare. If you qualify, the state pays a portion of the childcare cost directly to the provider, and you pay the difference (your "co-pay"). To apply, contact the Louisiana Department of Children and Family Services at 1-888-LAHELPU (1-888-524-3578) or visit your local DCFS office. The WBR Parish DCFS office is located at 610 N Alexander Ave in Port Allen.

How to Choose the Right Center

After checking the licensing record, here are the questions that matter most when visiting a childcare center in WBR:

  • What is the staff turnover rate? High turnover means your child is constantly adjusting to new caregivers. WBR centers tend to have more stable staffing than larger Baton Rouge operations, but ask directly.
  • Can you visit unannounced? Any center that discourages drop-in visits is a center you should think twice about. Good programs welcome parents at any time.
  • What happens when a child is sick? Ask about the illness policy -- fever thresholds, when children must stay home, and how the center handles an outbreak of something contagious.
  • What is the outdoor play schedule? Louisiana heat makes this tricky. Good centers adjust outdoor time to cooler parts of the day in summer and have shaded play areas.
  • How does the center handle discipline? Louisiana law prohibits corporal punishment in licensed childcare centers. Ask about the specific positive guidance strategies the center uses.
  • What meals and snacks are provided? Centers participating in the USDA Child and Adult Care Food Program (CACFP) provide nutritious meals at no extra cost. Ask if the center participates.
  • Is there a waitlist, and how long is it? Infant care waitlists in WBR can run three to six months. If you are expecting, start looking early in the second trimester.
Local Tip: Talk to other parents. WBR is a small community, and word of mouth is the most reliable review system. Ask at your church, at the pediatrician's office, or at the park. Parents who have had good (or bad) experiences are usually happy to share.

West Baton Rouge may not have the volume of childcare options that Baton Rouge offers, but the centers here benefit from the parish's tight-knit community. The staff knows the families, the families know each other, and the environment is closer to what many parents grew up with themselves. Start your search early, check the licensing records, visit in person, and trust your instincts. Your child's first classroom experience sets the tone for everything that follows.
