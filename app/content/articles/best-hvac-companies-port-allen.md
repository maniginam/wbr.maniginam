## Why HVAC Matters More in WBR

      If you have lived in West Baton Rouge Parish for even one summer, you know that air conditioning is not optional. The combination of extreme heat and oppressive humidity makes South Louisiana one of the most demanding climates in the country for HVAC systems.
      Here is what WBR homeowners deal with:
      
        
- **8-9 months of cooling season.** Your AC runs from April through November, and sometimes into December. That is a lot of wear and tear.
        
- **Extreme humidity.** Your HVAC system does not just cool your home -- it removes moisture. An undersized or poorly maintained system will leave your home feeling clammy even at low thermostat settings.
        
- **Mold and mildew risk.** Humidity control is critical in WBR homes. A failing HVAC system can lead to mold growth in ductwork, attics, and walls.
        
- **High energy bills.** Cooling accounts for the largest share of most WBR utility bills. An efficient system pays for itself over time.
        
- **Brief but real heating needs.** While winters are mild, there are stretches of cold weather in December through February where you need reliable heat.

## AC Repair: When to Call a Pro

      Not every HVAC issue requires a service call, but many do. Here is a guide to help you decide:
      
### Call an HVAC technician if:

      
        
- The system is blowing warm air
        
- You hear unusual noises (grinding, squealing, banging)
        
- You notice ice forming on the indoor or outdoor unit
        
- Water is leaking from the indoor unit
        
- The system runs constantly but never reaches the set temperature
        
- You smell something burning or musty when the system runs
        
- Your energy bill has spiked without explanation
        
- The system will not turn on at all
      
      
### Try these first before calling:

      
        
- **Check the thermostat settings.** Make sure it is set to "cool" and the temperature is set below the current room temperature.
        
- **Check the air filter.** A clogged filter is the single most common cause of HVAC problems. Replace it if it is dirty.
        
- **Check the breaker.** A tripped breaker can shut down the indoor unit, outdoor unit, or both.
        
- **Check the drain line.** Many WBR systems have a float switch on the condensate drain. If the line is clogged, the float switch shuts down the system to prevent water damage. You can often clear the line with a wet-dry vacuum.
      
      
        **Pro Tip:** In WBR's humid climate, condensate drain lines clog frequently. Pouring a cup of white vinegar down the drain line every few months helps keep it clear and can prevent unnecessary service calls.
      

      
## Choosing a New HVAC System

      If your system is 12-15 years old, requires frequent repairs, uses R-22 refrigerant (which has been phased out), or cannot keep your home comfortable, it is time to consider a replacement.
      A good HVAC company will perform a Manual J load calculation on your home before recommending a system size. This calculation accounts for:
      
        
- Square footage and layout
        
- Insulation levels (many older WBR homes are under-insulated)
        
- Window size, type, and orientation
        
- Ductwork condition and design
        
- Number of occupants
        
- Local climate data
      
      Never let a company size your new system based solely on square footage or by matching the old system's size. The old system may have been wrong from the start.

## Maintenance: Your Best Investment

      In Louisiana's demanding climate, HVAC maintenance is not optional -- it is what keeps your system running efficiently and extends its lifespan. Here is what a proper maintenance visit should include:
      
### Spring/Summer Tune-Up (Cooling)

      
        
- Check refrigerant levels and look for leaks
        
- Clean the outdoor condenser coils
        
- Inspect and clean the indoor evaporator coil
        
- Check the condensate drain and clear if needed
        
- Test electrical connections and capacitors
        
- Verify thermostat operation
        
- Inspect ductwork for leaks or damage
        
- Check airflow and measure temperature differential
      
      
### Fall/Winter Tune-Up (Heating)

      
        
- Inspect the heat exchanger for cracks (gas furnace)
        
- Test the ignition system
        
- Check the flue and venting
        
- Test safety controls
        
- Verify heat pump defrost cycle (if applicable)
        
- Replace the air filter
      
      Most HVAC companies in the WBR area offer maintenance plans that include two visits per year (spring and fall), priority scheduling, and discounts on repairs. These plans typically cost $150-$300 per year and are well worth it.

      
## Indoor Air Quality in WBR

      West Baton Rouge's humidity creates indoor air quality challenges that go beyond just temperature control:
      
        
- **Humidity control.** Your AC system is your primary dehumidifier. If your home feels clammy even when the AC is running, the system may be oversized (it cools too fast without removing enough moisture) or there may be ductwork issues.
        
- **Mold prevention.** Keep indoor humidity between 45-55%. A whole-house dehumidifier can supplement your HVAC system during the wettest months.
        
- **Air filtration.** Upgrading from a basic fiberglass filter to a MERV 11-13 pleated filter significantly improves air quality. Change it every 60-90 days, or monthly during peak cooling season.
        
- **UV lights.** UV germicidal lights installed in the air handler kill mold and bacteria growing on the evaporator coil. This is especially valuable in our humid climate.
        
- **Duct cleaning.** If you have never had your ducts cleaned and your home is more than 10 years old, it is worth considering. Mold, dust, and debris accumulate in WBR ductwork over time.
      

      
## Energy Efficiency Tips for WBR Homes

      Keeping your home cool in West Baton Rouge does not have to break the bank. Here are practical ways to reduce your cooling costs:
      
        
- **Set your thermostat to 76-78 degrees.** Every degree lower costs approximately 3-5% more on your cooling bill. Use ceiling fans to make the room feel 3-4 degrees cooler.
        
- **Seal your ductwork.** Leaky ducts in a hot WBR attic can waste 20-30% of your cooled air. Duct sealing is one of the highest-return efficiency improvements you can make.
        
- **Add attic insulation.** Many older WBR homes have inadequate attic insulation. Bringing it up to R-38 (the recommended level for our climate zone) makes a significant difference.
        
- **Use a programmable or smart thermostat.** Let the temperature rise a few degrees when you are at work and cool down before you get home.
        
- **Keep blinds and curtains closed on south and west-facing windows.** Solar heat gain through windows is a major cooling load in Louisiana.
        
- **Maintain your system.** A well-maintained system runs 10-15% more efficiently than a neglected one.
        
- **Consider a variable-speed system.** Modern variable-speed compressors and blowers adjust their output to match the load, providing better humidity control and lower energy use.
      

      
## How to Hire the Right HVAC Company

      When it is time to hire an HVAC company in West Baton Rouge, follow these steps:
      
        
- **Ask around locally.** Neighbors, co-workers, and the local community boards are great sources for referrals in Port Allen, Brusly, and Addis.
        
- **Verify the license.** Louisiana requires a mechanical contractor license for HVAC work. Check the state licensing board.
        
- **Get at least three written estimates for system replacement.** Make sure each company is quoting comparable equipment and includes the same scope of work.
        
- **Ask about load calculations.** Any company that quotes a new system without performing a Manual J calculation is cutting corners.
        
- **Inquire about warranties.** You want both a manufacturer equipment warranty (usually 5-10 years) and a labor warranty from the installer (1-2 years minimum).
        
- **Ask about maintenance plans.** A company that offers and encourages maintenance is one that cares about long-term customer relationships.
        
- **Check for permits.** HVAC installation requires a permit in WBR. Contact the Permit Office at (225) 336-2434 if you have questions.
        
- **Do not buy on price alone.** The cheapest bid often means cheaper equipment, shortcuts on installation, or both. A properly installed mid-range system will outperform a poorly installed premium system every time.
