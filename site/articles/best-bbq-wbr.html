Cou-Yon's Cajun Bar-B-Q: The West Side Standard

Port Allen, LA -- (225) 383-3227

If you ask anyone in West Baton Rouge where to get barbecue, the first two words out of their mouth are "Cou-Yon's." This Port Allen institution has built its reputation on doing two things at once: real, low-and-slow smoked barbecue, and the Cajun twist that makes South Louisiana barbecue its own animal. The name itself is a wink -- "couyon" is Cajun French for a foolish or playful person -- and the food backs up the swagger.

The smoker is the heart of the operation. Brisket is cooked until it slices clean and still holds a smoke ring; the ribs come off tender enough to pull from the bone but with enough bark to remind you they spent real time over wood. This is not sauce-drowned barbecue covering up a fast cook. The meat stands on its own, and the sauce is there to complement, not rescue.

What sets Cou-Yon's apart from a Texas or Carolina joint is the Cajun crossover. This is barbecue cooked by people who also know their way around a boudin link and a roux, and it shows. The result is a menu where smoked meat meets South Louisiana flavor, and the two were clearly made for each other.

What to Order at Cou-Yon's

Brisket: Order it sliced. It is the truest test of a barbecue kitchen, and Cou-Yon's passes. Get it as a plate with sides, or piled on a sandwich.

Ribs: The pork ribs are a parish favorite -- smoky, tender, and generously portioned. A rib plate with two sides is the safe order for a first visit.

Boudin-stuffed everything: This is where the Cajun-BBQ identity comes alive. Boudin worked into the menu in ways a non-Louisiana smokehouse would never think of. If there is a stuffed or boudin-laced special on the board, that is the order that tells you where you are eating.

The sides: Barbecue is only as good as the sides it travels with, and the Cajun-leaning sides here -- think dirty rice, beans, and the usual smokehouse staples -- hold their end up.

Local tip: Cou-Yon's runs popular at lunch, and the best cuts can sell out as the day goes on. Get there earlier rather than later, and call ahead at (225) 383-3227 for large or catering orders.

Smoked Meats at the Meat Markets

Not all great smoked meat in West Baton Rouge comes off a restaurant plate. The parish's Cajun meat markets are a parallel barbecue tradition -- smoked sausage, tasso, and seasoned meats you take home and finish yourself.

Benoit's Country Meat Block -- Addis

7251 Hwy 1 South, Addis, LA 70710 -- (225) 749-3869

Part meat market, part restaurant. The case is full of smoked sausage, seasoned and stuffed meats, and fresh cuts ready for your own pit. If you are doing the cooking this weekend, Benoit's is where you source the meat. The restaurant side serves Cajun plates from the same quality ingredients.

Bergeron's Boudin & Cajun Meats -- Port Allen

760 LA-415, Port Allen, LA 70767 -- (225) 338-0921

Better known for boudin and cracklins, Bergeron's also carries smoked sausage and seasoned meats that belong on any West Baton Rouge cookout. Grab a few links of smoked sausage to throw on the pit alongside whatever you are smoking.

BBQ Across the Bridge

When you want to expand the search, Baton Rouge is a short hop across the Mississippi River bridge. City Pork (with several Baton Rouge locations) is the best-known craft barbecue and smokehouse name in the metro area, doing brisket, pulled pork, and a rotating board of smoked specials. It is a ten-minute drive from Port Allen when you want a change of scenery -- but for west-side barbecue with Cajun soul, Cou-Yon's keeps people on this side of the river.
